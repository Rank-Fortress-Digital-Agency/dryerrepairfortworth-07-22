Why should you never wait to get your dryer repaired?


It’s no secret that appliances don’t last forever. And when they do go on the fritz, it can be tempting to put off repairs in favor of more important things. But there are a few excellent reasons why you should never wait to get your dryer repaired.

Your dryer is a fire hazard.

One of the most important reasons to promptly repair your dryer is that it poses a serious fire hazard. According to the National Fire Protection Association, clothes dryers are responsible for an estimated 15,500 fires yearly. And while that may seem like a small number, clothing dryer fires cause an estimated $238 million in property damage annually.

Dryers are a fire risk because lint buildup can easily ignite, and the high temperatures inside a dryer make the fire spread quickly. So if you notice that your dryer isn’t working as well as it used to or there’s excessive lint buildup, don’t wait to get it repaired.

Another important reason to keep your dryer in good working order is that it can save you money on your energy bills. A dryer that isn’t operating efficiently will use more energy, which means higher utility bills. So if you’re noticing that your energy bills have gone up, it could be due to a problem with your dryer.


Repairing your dryer can extend its lifespan. A well-maintained dryer will last longer than one that isn’t correctly cared for. And since dryers are not cheap to replace, it’s worth taking the time to repair them when needed.

Your dryer won’t be as efficient.

Another reason to promptly repair your dryer is that it won’t be as efficient, which means higher utility bills. When a dryer isn’t working correctly, it has to run longer to dry your clothes, which uses more energy and costs you more money.

Additionally, the problem will likely worsen if you don’t repair your dryer immediately. This can cause even more expensive repairs down the road or potentially lead to a dangerous fire hazard. So, it’s always best to take care of any dryer issues as soon as possible.

If you need help repairing your dryer, contact a professional appliance repair company like us. We’ll be happy to take a look and fix the problem so that your dryer runs efficiently and safely again.

Your clothes will suffer.

In addition to costing you more money, running a dryer that isn’t working correctly can also damage your clothes. If the dryer overloads the circuit, the heating element can overheat and cause your clothes to scorch or fire. And if the dryer isn’t getting hot enough, your clothes won’t dry properly, leading to mildew and mold growth.

If you notice your clothes take longer to dry than usual, or if they’re not getting dry, it’s time to call a dryer repair technician. A professional can troubleshoot the problem and make the necessary repairs. They may also be able to clean the lint trap and venting system, which can improve the dryer’s performance and prevent future problems.

Putting off repairs can also cause your dryer to break down sooner. That’s because the problem you’re ignoring will likely worsen over time, which can also cause other parts of the dryer to break down. So if you want your dryer to last as long as possible, don’t wait to get it repaired.

Getting your dryer repaired promptly is also essential because it can be a fire hazard. A dryer that isn’t working correctly can overheat and start a fire. So if you have any concerns about your dryer, don’t hesitate to call a repairer.

Of course, you can try to repair your dryer yourself, but it’s best to leave it to the professionals. They have the experience and the knowledge to fix the problem quickly and safely. So if you want your dryer to last, get it repaired as soon as possible.

You could void your warranty.

If your dryer is still under warranty, you may void the warranty by putting off repairs. Most manufacturers require that you have the dryer serviced by a qualified technician to maintain the warranty. Many appliance stores offer free dryer repair diagnosis. They will often waive the service fee if they find that the problem is a manufacturer’s defect.
